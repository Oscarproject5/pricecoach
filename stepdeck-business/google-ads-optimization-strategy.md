## STEP 3: New Customer Qualification Script (With Ad Costs in Mind)

When a lead calls from your ad, use this script:

**You:** "Thanks for calling! I saw you came from our website. Are you looking at our Load-Ready service or Full-Service coordination?"

*(This reminds them they already saw two price points)*

**Customer:** "Uh, what's the difference?"

**You:** "Load-Ready is for customers who have their own forklift or loading equipment on-site and ready when I arrive. That service starts at $600 for our 30-mile radius. Full-Service is when we help coordinate loading equipment or services, and that starts at $900. Which situation fits your needs?"

---

### If They Push Back on Price

**Customer:** "That's more expensive than I expected. I saw other companies for $500."

**You:** "I understand. Here's what's different: I run a professional operation with reliable equipment and service. My Load-Ready rate of $600 ensures I show up on time, your load gets hauled safely, and there are no surprises. The $500 companies you're seeing might not account for all their costs - or they might keep you waiting for days.

If you're looking for the absolute cheapest option, I might not be the best fit. But if you value your time and need this done right the first time, I'm your guy. What matters more to you - lowest price or reliable service?"

**Let them decide. If they choose price, let them go.**

---

### If They Say "$600 Is Too Much"

**You:** "I totally understand budget matters. Let me ask - do you have loading equipment on-site and ready to go? Because if not, you're actually looking at our Full-Service tier at $900, not $600."

*(Often this makes $600 seem more reasonable)*

**If they still balk:**

**You:** "No problem. I appreciate you reaching out. If the other company doesn't work out or you decide you want professional service, feel free to call back. Good luck with your haul!"

**End the call. Don't chase.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

### "What if I can't get enough leads at $600?"

Then you have a marketing problem, not a pricing problem.

**Solutions:**
- Improve your Google Ads targeting
- Add local SEO (Google Business Profile)
- Ask happy customers for referrals
- Join local business groups
- Partner with equipment rental companies

But going back to $500 isn't the answer. You can't grow at $500 with $100 CAC.

---

### "What if competitors are still at $450-500?"

Let them be. They're either:
1. Not accounting for their real costs (and will go out of business)
2. Running a different business model than you
3. Lying about their prices (happens more than you think)

You're running a PROFESSIONAL operation with $2,000/month in marketing costs. You can't compete on price. You compete on:
- Reliability
- Professionalism
- Responsiveness
- Quality of service

---

### "Should I tell customers why my price is higher?"

Not usually. But if they ask directly:

**Customer:** "Why are you more expensive than [competitor]?"

**You:** "That's a fair question. I run a professional operation with commercial insurance, maintained equipment, and reliable service. I show up when I say I will, your load gets there safely, and you can track me in real-time. Some companies cut costs by skipping insurance, using older equipment, or overbooking their schedule.

I'm not the cheapest option - I'm the professional option. If lowest price is your priority, I totally understand, but if reliability and peace of mind matter, I'm worth the difference."

---

### "What if my Google Ads don't improve?"

Give it 30 days. If after 30 days you're still getting poor quality leads:

**Possible issues:**
1. Your ad copy isn't filtering well enough
2. Your landing page doesn't pre-qualify
3. Your keywords are too broad
4. Your service area is too competitive
5. You need to try other marketing channels

**Next steps:**
- Consider hiring a Google Ads specialist ($500-1,000 to audit and optimize)
- Try local SEO and Google Business Profile (free)
- Ask for referrals from past good customers
- Partner with equipment rental companies who can refer you

But don't go back to $500 flat pricing. That's a death spiral.
